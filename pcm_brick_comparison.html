24-hour heat cycle

Morning · 6am–10am
Outside temp rises above PCM melt threshold (23–27°C). PCM begins absorbing heat — indoor temp stays level while walls “charge.”
Peak heat · 10am–4pm
PCM fully melting. Q = m × L absorbed silently. Conventional brick transmits full 38°C+ indoors. PCM brick holds indoor at ≈28°C.
Cooling · 4pm–10pm
Outdoor temp drops. PCM holds latent heat — room stays warmer than outside without heating systems.
Night · 10pm–6am
PCM solidifies, releasing ΔHfusion back into the room. ΔS = ΔH / Tmelt. Fully reset for the next cycle.

Why PCM bricks last

  • Micro-encapsulation seals PCM in polymer shell — no leakage over cycles
  • No moving parts or mechanical wear — purely thermodynamic operation
  • Phase transitions are reversible by nature — tested 1000+ cycles
  • Structural integrity maintained at 20% PCM content by weight

Conventional brick limitations

  • Only sensible heat storage — 5–14× lower energy density
  • Full heat transmission — no thermal buffering capability
  • No mechanism to shift or delay heat load
  • Requires HVAC to compensate for poor thermal performance
