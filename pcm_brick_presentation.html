01 — The Idea

A BRICK THAT
THINKS IN HEAT

A phase-change material (PCM) — like paraffin wax or a salt hydrate — is encapsulated inside a honeycomb cavity within a standard building brick. When ambient temperature rises, the PCM melts, absorbing large amounts of energy without temperature change. At night, it solidifies, releasing that stored energy back — keeping rooms naturally stable.

☀️
Daytime
Heat enters wall
PCM melts → absorbs energy
Interior stays cool
🔥
🌙
Nighttime
Temperature drops
PCM solidifies → releases heat
Interior stays warm

The secret: latent heat stores 5–14× more energy than sensible heat alone.

Key Thermodynamic Equations
Q = m × L
Q = heat stored (kJ)  |  m = mass of PCM (kg)  |  L = latent heat of fusion (kJ/kg)
Paraffin wax: L ≈ 200 kJ/kg   ·   Salt hydrates: L ≈ 150–300 kJ/kg
ΔS = ΔHfusion / Tmelt
Entropy change during phase transition — quantifiable and directly linked to thermodynamic efficiency.

04 — Applications

WHERE THESE
BRICKS SHINE

PCM bricks work best where outdoor temperatures swing significantly between day and night — maximizing the charge/discharge cycle.

🏜️

Hot & Arid Climates

Arizona, Middle East, India, North Africa — huge diurnal swings make every cycle count. AC costs cut dramatically.

🏘️

Affordable Housing

In developing regions where AC is unaffordable, passive cooling saves lives during heat events — not just money.

🏫

Schools & Clinics

Public buildings with large occupant loads benefit most — lower HVAC bills free budget for education and healthcare.

🏗️

New Construction

Easiest to integrate during new builds — adds minimal structural weight and drops overall energy code requirements.

🔄

Retrofits

PCM panels and boards can be added to existing interior walls — no demolition needed for existing buildings.

⚡

Grid Load Shifting

Absorbing daytime heat reduces peak electricity demand — directly reducing strain on the power grid at crunch hours.

05 — Peer-Reviewed Research

BACKED BY
SCIENCE

ScienceDirect · Energy Storage · 2025

Enhancing Thermal Performance in Concrete Bricks with Phase Change Materials

Numerical study using ANSYS Fluent comparing RT37, RT42, and composite PCMs embedded in brick cavities.

🔬 Key finding: RT37 PCM lowered indoor temperature by 6.68°C, reduced heat gain by 4.65W, and cut CO₂ emissions by 0.112 kg/day per brick.
ScienceDirect · Energy Storage · 2025

Eco-Friendly Compressed Earth Bricks Enhanced with Micro-Encapsulated PCM

Experimental study varying PCM content 0–20% by weight, measuring mechanical and thermal performance across 5 melt-solidification cycles.

🔬 Key finding: 20% mPCM content increased heat capacity ~79% and improved latent heat storage by a factor of 15.5, while maintaining structural requirements.
MDPI Materials · 2025

Phase Change Materials in Residential Buildings: Challenges, Opportunities, and Performance

Systematic literature review (PRISMA guidelines) covering 2015–2025 studies on PCM integration in walls, floors, ceilings, and windows.

🔬 Key finding: Optimized PCM integration can reduce building energy consumption by up to 30% and improve indoor thermal comfort.
ScienceDirect · Energy Buildings · 2022

Year-Long Energy Analysis of Building Brick Filled with Phase Change Materials

Long-term simulation comparing enthalpy-porosity and simplified models for PCMs in cylindrical brick cavities across full annual climate cycles.

🔬 Key finding: Single-layer PCM bricks reduced maximum temperature by up to 9.5°C; double-layer reduced heat transfer by 60%.
ScienceDirect · Review · 2023

Comprehensive Review: PCM Integration in Building Bricks — Methods, Performance, Applications

Covers macro-encapsulation in bricks, wallboards, and storage tanks. Introduces topology optimization for improved heat transfer structures.

🔬 Key finding: Wall bricks account for 25% of building energy consumption — PCM integration is one of the highest-leverage intervention points available.
